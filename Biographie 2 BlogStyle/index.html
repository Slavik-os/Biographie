Who is she ?

Joanne Rowling , better known by her pen name J. K. Rowling, is a British author, philanthropist, film producer, television producer, and screenwriter.

Birth and family

Rowling's sister Dianne was born at their home when Rowling was 23 months old.
The family moved to the nearby village of Winterbourne when Rowling was four.
As a child, Rowling often wrote fantasy stories which she frequently read to her sister .

Education

As a child, Rowling attended St Michael's Primary School,
a school founded by abolitionist William Wilberforce and education reformer Hannah More.
Her headmaster at St Michael's, Alfred Dunn, has been suggested as the inspiration
for the Harry Potter headmaster Albus Dumbledore.
She attended secondary school at Wyedean School and College.
